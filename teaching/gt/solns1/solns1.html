You’ll mostly need the following facts from the lectures to answer the homework questions.

  1. (Algebra II fact) Let f ∈ ℤ[x] be primitive (i.e. the gcd of all the coefficients is 1). Then f is irreducible in ℚ[x] if and only if it is irreducible in ℤ[x].
  2. If α is algebraic over a field K then its minimal polynomial over K is defined as the monic polynomial m ∈ K[x] of smallest possible degree such that m(α) = 0. Moreover, the minimal polynomial of α over K is the unique monic irreducible polynomial m ∈ K[x] such that m(α) = 0.
  3. Let α be algebraic over K having minimal polynomial m ∈ K[x] of degree d. Then [K(α) : K] = d. Moreover, a K-basis for K(α) is 1,α,…,αd−1.
  4. The tower law: if M∕L∕K then [M : K] = [M : L][L : K]. From the proof of the tower law we know that if {mi} is a basis for M∕L and {ℓj} is a basis for L∕K then {miℓj} is a basis for M∕K.
